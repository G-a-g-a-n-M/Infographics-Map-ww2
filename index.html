World War II Timeline

Pre-War Events (1918-1939)

  • 1918: Treaty of Versailles ends World War I, imposing harsh penalties on Germany
  • 1933: Adolf Hitler becomes Chancellor of Germany, begins to rearm and pursue aggressive expansion
  • 1935: Germany remilitarizes the Rhineland, violating Treaty of Versailles
  • 1936: Italy invades Ethiopia
  • 1937: Japan invades China
  • 1938: Germany annexes Austria, Czechoslovakia
  • 1939: Germany invades Poland, prompting declarations of war by France and the United Kingdom

Early War (1939-1941)

  • September 1939: Germany invades Poland
  • May 1940: Germany invades France, Belgium, the Netherlands, and Luxembourg
  • June 1940: Italy declares war on France and the United Kingdom
  • July 1940: Germany begins bombing British cities
  • December 1940: Japan signs Tripartite Pact with Germany and Italy
  • June 1941: Germany launches Operation Barbarossa, invading the Soviet Union

Mid-War (1941-1943)

  • December 1941: Japan attacks Pearl Harbor, drawing the United States into the war
  • 1942: Germany and Italy suffer defeats in North Africa and the Soviet Union
  • 1943: Allies gain control of North Africa and Italy, Germany begins to lose ground

Aftermath

  • 1945: World War II officially ends with the surrender of Germany and Japan
  • Estimated 20 million soldiers and 40 million civilians killed during the war.
